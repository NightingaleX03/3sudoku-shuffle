MAKING A MOVE
Gameplay

When placing a tile, there should be no repetition of a color, shape or number on the location's row, column and 3x3 block.

There are 2 valid reasons to place a tile:

  1. There is a spot where only one of the tiles in the rack can be placed. Any other tile will repeat a color, shape or number.
  2. A tile may be placed on only one location. Anywhere else on the puzzle grid, the tile will repeat a color, shape of number.

Avoid guessing. It will be difficult to trace a mistake and undo the moves.

TECHNIQUES
Gameplay

Fill in a blank in a row, column or 3x3 block:

  1. Search for a row, column or 3x3 block with 8 clues.
  2. Rack by number. Count off the numbers in the row, column or block.
  3. Choose from the rack the missing color.
  4. There may be more than one tile of a color, then choose the missing shape

Rearrange the racks by color to find a single tile of a color on the racks. Find the location on the grid where that color won't repeat. Sort by Shape and then Number

SUDOKU SCANNING
Techniques

Look for a number that occurs 2 times on the top 3 rows.

  1. Check if the number can only be placed on one square on the row.
  2. Choose from the tiles in the rack. If only one is good, place it.

Do as above for the middle 3 rows, and for the bottom 3 rows.

Do as above for the left 3 columns, the middle 3 columns and the right 3 columns.

Do as the three above for colors and shapes.

FULL SCAN
Techniques

Rank by color

  1. Choose a color with a few tiles on the rack (1, 2, or 3).
  2. Move the leftmost tile on the rack to the end. This is reminder of the color to work with.
  3. Find the squares on the grid where the color may be placed. Note that: the number of tiles to place is equal to the number of rows, columns and blocks without that color.
  4. Move the bullseye around the puzzle to better visualize where the tiles may be placed.
  5. Figure out if the tiles can be placed, without guessing. Place them accordingly.

Do as above for other "short" colors.

Do as the two above by shape and by number.

POTHOLES
Techniques

This requires a work area among the racks for temporary placement of candidate tiles. Rack by number.

  1. Find a row, column or block with a few "potholes" (empty squares).
  2. Count off the missing numbers.
  3. Find the candidate tiles in the racks.
  4. Move them to the work area.
  5. Find the combination of tiles that "are Sudoku".
  6. Place the tiles in the row, column or block.
  7. Rack by number to return the unused tiles to the proper racks.
